Spanish-American War

The United States declared war on Spain in 1898 after an American battleship exploded in Spanish territory.

Roosevelt, eager for battle, formed the First US Volunteer Cavalry Regiment with Colonel Leonard Wood.

The Regiment became known as "The Rough Riders" and thousands of people from all backgrounds came across the country to join.

The Rough Riders were sent to Cuba where Roosevelt was promoted to Colonel

He became famous for leading the charge at the Battle of San Juan Hill, which was the bloodiest battle of the war. (Roosevelt and the Rough Riders can be seen on top of San Juan Hill in the top photo)

The United States had won the war soon after with Roosevelt and the Rough Riders becoming national heroes. He said the battle was, "the great day of my life."

Roosevelt was posthumously awarded the Medal of Honor for his actions in 2001.
