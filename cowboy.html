North Dakota

Roosevelt built Elkhorn Ranch in 1884 in the Dakota Territory, hoping to become a successful cattle rancher.

Here he learned how to ride western style and hunt on the banks of the Missouri River.

When thieves stole his river boat, Roosevelt and two ranch hands built another boat and apprehended them down river

(Roosevelt can be seen guarding the boat thieves in the last photo)

After a bad winter killed all of his cattle, he moved back east.

Today, the area is known as Theodore Roosevelt National Park.
